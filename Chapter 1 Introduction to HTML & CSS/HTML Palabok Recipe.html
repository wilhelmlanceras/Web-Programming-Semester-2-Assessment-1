Palabok Recipe

by Lanceras Family

Since 1985

Palabok is a beloved Filipino dish made with soft rice noodles, a savory sauce, and topped with crispy chicharon, smokey tinapa, boiled eggs, and fresh spring onions. This version of Palabok is lovingly prepared by the Lanceras family, especially by Lola Venie who made it from scratch, with love, and filled with fond memories.

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 kgs Super Q Special Palabok noodles
  • 3 kilos of pork head or kasim
  • 2 heads of garlic
  • 2 onions
  • 3-4 ladles of soy sauce
  • 2 tsp of black pepper
  • 1 small sachet of Magic Sarap
  • Tinapa (smoked fish)
  • 6 pcs of Knorr chicken cubes
  • 1 cup of flour
  • 12 sachets of Boston Gawgaw (cornstarch)
  • Atsuete for coloring (annatto seeds)
  • Calamansi (Filipino lime)
  • 15 eggs (hard-boiled)
  • Spring onion (1 ½ bunches)
  • Chicharon (crushed)

Method:

  1. Soak the noodles in water for 30 minutes to soften.
  2. Boil the noodles for 20 minutes (start timing when water is boiling). Once done, let it stay in the pot for another 15 minutes then drain.
  3. For the pork, boil it for 1 hour until tender. Add salt to season the pork. Once cooked, cool and chop into small pieces.
  4. In a separate pan, sauté garlic and onions. Add chopped pork, soy sauce (about 3-4 ladles), black pepper, and Magic Sarap. Cook and mix for 5 to 10 minutes.
  5. For the tinapa, remove the bones, shred it and toast in a dry pan until lightly brown.
  6. For the sauce, use the broth from the pork. Add Knorr chicken cubes, Magic Sarap, soy sauce, and atsuete for coloring. Mix in flour and cornstarch mixed with water to thicken the sauce.
  7. Assemble the Palabok by placing the noodles on a plate, pouring the sauce on top, and adding the pork mixture, tinapa, boiled eggs, chicharon, spring onions, and calamansi on the side.
